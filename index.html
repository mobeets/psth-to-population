Neural population dynamics

Visualize the time-varying activity of three neurons in population space.

Below, each neuron's time-varying firing rate (or PSTH) in response to a stimulus θ is modeled as:
The idea is that when a stimulus (θ) is shown, a neuron's firing rate gradually increases from spontaneous firing (r_spont) to evoked firing (r_evoked). Above, f_τ(t) is a gain signal that ramps up from 0 to 1, so that τ controls how quickly the firing rate responds to the stimulus. Each neuron has a generic response to the presence of a stimulus (r_generic), regardless of which stimulus it is. The other component of the neuron's response is cosine-tuned, where the preferred stimulus (θ_pref) for each of three neurons is 45º, 135º, and 180º, respectively.
